Sustainable Aviation: Evaluating Green Hydrogen and SAFs

Overview:
Sustainable aviation is a critical challenge in the face of climate change, particularly with the anticipated rise in global air travel. The aviation industry's pursuit of net-zero emissions has largely focused on Sustainable Aviation Fuels (SAFs), which can be blended with conventional Jet A fuel to reduce carbon emissions.

However, while SAFs offer significant improvements over traditional fuels, they are not entirely clean. Emissions still occur during the feedstock production and processing stages, raising questions about their long-term viability as a truly sustainable solution.

Green Hydrogen: A Clean Alternative

Green hydrogen is as a promising, fully clean alternative fuel for aviation. Produced using renewable energy sources, green hydrogen emits no carbon during its lifecycle, making it a potential game-changer for sustainable flight.

Purpose of This Project:
- Analyze the projected production trends of green hydrogen.
- Compare these trends against the growing demand for SAFs.
- Evaluate the feasibility, scalability, and infrastructure needs for green hydrogen adoption in aviation.
  
By investigating both SAFs and green hydrogen, this project seeks to inform strategies that can support a sustainable and climate-resilient future for air travel.
